This stage deserves attention

The Flat Stage Is Where Many Wives Start Leaving Emotionally.

That is the hard part about this kind of marriage. It does not look urgent from the outside.

You are still married. You still function. You still smile at church. He may genuinely believe everything is fine.

But slow drift has a way of becoming normal. Ordinary evenings become ordinary months. Little bids for connection get missed. The phone becomes easier than the conversation. Practical partnership replaces tenderness.

69%

Stanford sociologist Michael Rosenfeld found women wanted the breakup in 69% of the marriages that ended in his analysis.

1 in 4

In one national study, around one in four married people had thought about divorce in the previous six months.

16%

Barna reported that practising Christians and non-Christians were equally likely to say they had been divorced.

The point is not to scare you. It is to name something many women know in their bodies before they can explain it: the "we're fine" stage deserves attention before distance hardens into discontent.

This does not mean you are heading for divorce.

It means your marriage is worth tending while there is still goodwill, affection, history, faith and love to work with.

You might already feel an objection rising here: "Why is this on me?"

That is understandable. Many women were taught by family, culture or church that a husband should be the one making the effort, leading the way or noticing the distance first. In some marriages, that does happen.

But in many real marriages, it is the wife who feels the drift first, names it first and starts looking for a way to respond first. That does not make the marriage your responsibility to carry. It simply means you may be the first one awake to what is happening.

Sources: Michael Rosenfeld, Institute for Family Studies/BYU divorce ideation research, and Barna Marriage and Divorce Trends 2025.

Real sample day

Day 6: Kindness

Scripture anchor: "Therefore, as God's chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience." Colossians 3:12

Choose one option for today:

  1. Pray: Pray that God would reveal anything in your heart that has hardened or closed off, and help your marriage become a place of comfort and emotional rest.
  2. Reset your posture: Reflect on your attitude toward your spouse today and ask God to help you respond with more patience, kindness and humility.
  3. Do one kind thing: Do one small helpful task for your spouse without pointing it out.
  4. Talk about what has been going well: Spend a few minutes sharing what has felt good in your relationship lately. Keep the focus positive.
  5. Kindness in action night: Plan a simple evening where you actively do small kind things for your spouse, then sit together and tell them what you appreciate about them.

The app cannot guarantee how any one day will land. The purpose is to help you practise faithful, loving action consistently, in a way that is realistic for your life.

Got Questions?

"My husband probably will not do this with me."

That is okay. The app is designed so one motivated spouse can begin privately. If he wants to join, your subscription includes a link you can send him so both apps sync to the same day.

"I do not want to feel like this is all on me."

One spouse choosing to begin is not the same as one spouse carrying the whole marriage. You are responsible for your choices. Your husband is responsible for his. This gives you a faithful way to practise your part without pretending you control the outcome.

"We are doing okay. Do we actually need this?"

This is an everyday tool for marriages that are good but want to be tended with more intention. The marriages most vulnerable to slow drift are often the ones where nothing seems wrong enough to act on.

"I have bought Christian marriage resources before and never finished them."

This is different in structure. You open the app, read the verse, choose one action and do it. That is the whole daily cycle. There is no programme to work through in order.
